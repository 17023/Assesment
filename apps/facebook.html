The page at the top that is blue is the page you're currently on, this can help you distinguish what one you have open.


Friend Requests.

The friend request page is where you you can find four of the following aspects:

Requests: If you click this button, a page will be displayed with the people who wish to be friends with you. You have the option to choose whether you want to accept or decline their request, don't worry though if you choose to decline their requests they won't be notified.

All Friends: This button will bring you to a page of all the people you are friends with on Facebook. On this page you can choose to sort your friends either by default, newest or oldest. You can also search through your friends, if your trying to find someone.

Search: The search button functions the same way as the one on the home page, for further detail click the search button on the image below.

People You May Know: Below this header is people you may know that have a Facebook account, they typically appear when you have mutual friends. Clicking "Add Friends" will send a requests to them and clicking "Remove" will remove them for this page.


Notifications.

This page has a all notifications you recieve from Facebook. You can get notifications for friend requests, comments on your post and when other people tagging/mentioning you in their post. Notifications that have a light blue tint to their background means you have not seen it and clicking the notification will make this disapear.

If you click the three dots next to the notifications you will be brought up with these options, allowing you to delete the notifications, customise your notifications and report any issues you have.

Search.

The search button is pretty simple to use, clicking it will display your keyboard. You are then able to search for people you want to friend, groups you want to join or pages to like/follow. Your recent searches and pages you have visted will be displayed under the search bar, to clear these click "See All" then "Clear All".

Profile.

Your profile is how other Facebook users perceive you. You are able to change your profile picture, cover image and details that are describe you.

Use the following buttons to navigate between the different functions of your profile.

Cover Image: This is the image that is displayed above your profile picture. People are able to view it when they vist your page.

View Profile Cover: This will display your image in full screen, you are then able to see what people commented on it when you posted it and who liked it. To exit this photo, simply click your phones back arrow.

Upload Photo: When you click this button you are able to change the image to one in your phones gallery. You may want to take into consideration that when you change your image Facebook will create a post that your friends can see.

Select Photo on Facebook: This button also allows you to pick a cover image, however these photos are ones that you have uploaded to Facebook.

Create Cover Collage: This allows you to pick multiple photos that will then be placed into a collage for your cover image.

Select Artwork: These are a selection of cover images you can use that has been provided by Facebook.

Profile Picture: This picture is displayed in two places, the first being on your profile, and the second is when you make a post, like/comment on someones post.

Add Frame: This allows you to place a frame on your profile picture. When you click this button you will be brought to a page with frames used by your friends and a search bar for you to find new frames.

Take New Profile Video: Taking a new profile video will display your camera for you to take a video to use for you profile picyure. You may want to take into consideration that when you change your video Facebook will create a post that your friends can see.

Select Profile Video: Clicking this will allow you to add a video to your profile picture. You may want to take into consideration that when you change your video Facebook will create a post that your friends can see.

Select Profile Picture: Clicking this will allow you to change your profile picture. You may want to take into consideration that when you change your image Facebook will create a post that your friends can see.

View Profile Picture: This will display your image in full screen, you are then able to see what people commented on it when you posted it and who liked it. To exit this photo, simply click your phones back arrow.

Make Avatar Profile Picture: This allows you to dress up an animated character that looks like you to use as your profile picture.

About You: Your about you section of your profile has many features that you can choose to fill out or not.

You are able to display where you work/worked, schools you attended, places lived, contact information, gender, birthday, who your interested in, other names you use, relationships and family members. You can change these as many times as you would like, as well as changing who can view this information whether it be to the public, your friends, friends and their friends or only yourself


Write Post.

When you click the "What's on your mind?" box on your home page, you are brought to a new area that allows you to type or click on of the following options.

...

◯ Photo/Video: Attach a photo or video to your post.

◯ Tag People: Mentioning other people on your post will notify them and be displayed on their profile meaning their friends can like uyour post.

◯ Feeling/Activity: Share with your friends how you are feeling or what your doing at the moment.

◯ Check In: Add a location to your post, to share where you have been with your friends.

◯ Background Colour: Change the background of your post, this can include the ones with a solid colour or ones with different shapes.

◯ GIF: Select a moving image to post.

◯ Raise Money: Raise money for your friends, someone else or organisation you would like.

◯ Host a Q&A: Get your friends to ask questions about a topic and you can answer the ones choose to.

Post Story.

Stories are a way to post photos, short video, voting polls and more for a limited amount of time. Stories are avaliable for your friends to see for 24 hours, after this time it will disapear. The following features are what type of stories you can post.

Text: This is just plain text on a coloured background of your choice. You are also able to change the font of your text.

Music: Select a song to play while your friends view your story, you can also add text, stickers and colours.

Green Screen: This alloes you to place different backgrounds behind you. After taking the photo or video you can decorate it with other features.

Boomerang: This is very short video that is played in a constant loop.

Selfie: Take a photo of yourself or someone your with, that has a filter on top. A filter is like an accessory that your camera places on you in your phone.

Poll: This allows you to post a voting poll where you can ask questions, preferences or something that can be answered with two options.

Mood: This is posting GIFs that have been categorised into different types of moods.


View friends story.

When your friends have posted a story it will be displayed at the top of your homepage. You are able to see their post without clicking it, however, for a more clearer view or if your friend has posted more than one story you will have to click it. When on it you can reply or react to it, and if you would like to exit it there is a cross button in the top right that closes the story.


Friends Post.

When your friends post something new on Facebook it will be displayed on your home page. These posts can include; photos, videos, links or simply just text. Your friends profile picture, name and when they made the post will be displayed on the top right of their post. They are also able to tag other people, or a location they were at, this will be displayed next to their name.

Sometimes you may see posts from people you aren't friends with, this is becasue a friend of yours has either been tagged or commented on their post.

There are a couple of ways for you to interact with your friends post including:

- The thumbs up button allows you to like your their post, if you hold down this button other options will be displayed for you. You are also able to love, laugh, cry, etc about their post, these are displayed as different emojis.

- Clicking the comment button allows you to comment on someones post, this can be with text, GIF or a sticker which are all located above your keyboard.

- Sharing your post to others will send this via messenger, you are able to send this to any friend you like.
