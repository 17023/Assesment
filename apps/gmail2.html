Go Back.

This Button is relatively simple, clicking it will bring you back to your Inbox. Nothing will happen to the email, unless this was your first time reading the email it will be marked as read.


Archive.

Archieving your email is kind of like deleting the email, it will be removed from your inbox and placed into your "All Mail" folder. It will stay in the folder untill you either delete it or the sender replies to the email, which it will then re-appear in your inbox. You can also archive your email while you're on the inbox tab, by sliding the email to the left.


Delete.

This button is pretty self explanatory, when you click it you will delete the email. If you wish to restore this email you have 30 days untill google permanently deletes it. To restore your email, go to the trash/bin tab in the side bar of your inbox.


Mark as Unread

If you have opened the email but wish to mark it as unread, clicking this button will mark it as unread. The sender won't be notified, so you can read and unread the email as many times as you want.

The image above is the difference between unread and read emails in your inbox. If the text is bold you have either not read the email or you have marked it as unread.


More Options.

When you click these three dots, a tab is displayed below. There are options you are able to click, which have each been explained below.

Move to - Allows you to move your email into different folders for a more organised inbox.

Snooze - This removes your email from your inbox and places it into the snoozed folder untill you need it again.

Mark as not important - By defult all emails you recieve are automatically marked as important and placed in your important folder on the side bar. Clicking this button will mark them as unimportant, which means they will be removed from your important folder

Mute - If you're recieving replies from an email and wish for them to stop, you are able to archieve this message via the mute button. This will stop the replies without deleting the messages.

Print - If you want a paper copy of the email for whatever reason, clicking this button will allow you to print it.

Report spam - Reporting an email as spam will send any further emails from this sender to your spam folder, which gets deleted after thirty days.

Help and feedback - If you are confused about any functions of Gmail, clicking this button will bring you to Googles help page for more detailed explainations.


Sender.

This is where you can see a few details the emails sender. The circle to the left is their profile picture and next to that is their name. You can also see the time/date of when the email was sent and if it was sent to others as well.


Reply.

This will bring up a new page which will look like your typing a new email, however, some of the parts have already been filled in.

Clicking the word "Reply" at the top will bring down three options:

Reply: This will reply to just the sender of the message.

Reply all: If the message had been sent to multiple people, clicking "Reply all" will send your reply to everyone.

Forward: This allows you to send a copy of the email to someone else. If you choose this option you will have to enter the name of the desired recipient in the "To" section.

If you click the three horizontal buttons at the bottom, a copy of the original email will be displayed.

Reply all: If the message had been sent to multiple people, clicking "Reply all" will allow you to send a reply to everyone.

Message.

This is the message itself, it's what the sender wanted to you to know. There's not much of an explanation need for this part, the only thing you can do with it is read it.


Attached file.

If someone has attached a file to the email it will be either displayed like this, or the like the image below. If it's displayed like this it will typically be a file from a Google application(e.g. Docs, Slide, Sheets, etc). Clicking the file link will bring you to the document which you can either view, comment or edit depending on what permission the owner has given you.


Image.

It won't always be an image attached like this, it could also be a document, video, etc.

If you want to download the file to you phone, clicking this button will automatically download it.

If you want to save the file to your Google Drive, clicking this button will automatically save it.
