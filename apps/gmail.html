Side Bar. (Three Lines)

When you click the side bar a tab will appear from the left with the following options for you to click. To get back to your inbox from either this tab or if you have clicked one of the options, just click the button that says "Inbox" in this tab.

Starred: This is where all of the emails that you have starred will appear(they will still be in your inbox though). Starred emails can help you distinguish between important and unimportant emails.

Snoozed: This allows you to to see emails you have temporarily removed.

Inportant: This folder places emails Google thinks maybe important to you. They do this by placing emails from frequent contacts or from keywords in the email.

Sent: Is the all the emails that you have sent. This can be helpful for you to determine if an email actually sends, if it doesn't it may appear in your drafts or outbox folder.

Scheduled: When composing an email you are able to set to be delivered at a certain time, so this is the folder they will reamin in untill the specified time.

Outbox: This displays all your emails that in the process of sending. This means that the recipient will not have recieved it untill it leaves this folder and appears in the sent folder.

Drafts: If you are writing an email and decide to finish it at a later time it is automatically saved in your Drafts folder. These emails won't be deleted after a certain amount of time, so you can leave them in there as long as you like.

All Mail: This is pretty similar to your inbox, however, it also contains sent, archieved, promotial and social emails. Most use this folder as a way to view their archieved emails.

Spam: Google automatically sorts between actual emails and ones they assume is junk. Spam emails will be automatically deleted after 30 days, however, there is a "Empty Spam now" if you wish to do it sooner.

Bin/Trash: All emails you have deleted will appear here. If you wish to resore the email, after clicking the email there should be three vertical dots in the right hand corner, in this a "Move to" button, after selecting this you will be able to move it to your inbox.


Search Mail.

If you wish to find an email in your inbox but are unable to, using this Search bar will help.

You are able to search using the name/email of the sender or key words you remember from the email. You should refrain from using long scentences, as it will make it harder for you to find the email.


Account.

This is your account, whatever you have chosen as your profile picture will be displayed here. Clicking this will bring up:

◯ Any other accounts you are signed into for you to access. Changing accounts will not sign you out of the account your on now, so you are able to return to this one afterwards.

◯ Adding other accounts. If you click this you then go through the processes of logging into another one of you accounts, which afterwards shoulds appear below your current account.

◯ Managing your accounts on your device will bring you to your settings, where your able to remove any unwanted accounts from your device.

Compose.

This is the button you click if you want to write an email, the following image is what will appear after you click compose(minus the red text).

When filling in the Recipient information, you have to know the persons entire email address just entering their name won't work(unless you have frequently contacted them or their in you google contacts). The subject should be the main reason for the email and shouldn't be to long(typiclly 25-30 characters). Where it says "Compose email" is where you write the message itself.

 The paperclip in the top right allows you to attach a document, picture or video from your device/google drive. If you add large files this will the time it takes the email to send slower.

The arrow is the send button, only click this if your email is complete as you can't take it back after it's sent.


Mail.

Meet.

Google Meet is a type video chat with multiple other people. If you would like to host a meeting, you will be given a code to send to other people that would like to join your meeting. The code acts as a key of some sorts, it's what they enter so they can join your specific meeting. If you are given the code and would like to join someone elses meeting click the "Join with a code" button, which you then enter the code you recieved.
